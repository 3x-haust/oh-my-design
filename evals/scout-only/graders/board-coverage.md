# Grader: Reference Coverage Contract

Check whether the scout assembled enough independent, measured evidence to answer the brief,
without rewarding capture volume or gallery prestige.

## Pass criteria

- The board names the design decisions and required components/states it needed to research.
- Every applicable area has at least one useful evidence path: domain expectations,
  competitors/alternatives, user or community language, typography/voice, relevant motion,
  and uncertain component anatomy.
- Each retained reference names the question it answers, transferable measurements or
  principles, contradictions, source trust, uncertainty, and its likely downstream use.
- Related or derivative pages are treated as one evidence family rather than independent
  corroboration; obvious duplicates and kin at similarity `>= .85` are removed.
- Irrelevant categories and unresolved gaps are explicitly marked with reasons.
- The handoff contains sanitized measurements and principles, not screenshots or imitation
  instructions.

## Fail criteria

- Completion is justified by a query, capture, famous-site, or award-gallery count.
- A required component or decision has no evidence and no recorded gap.
- Prestige, popularity, or visual resemblance substitutes for source trust and relevance.
- References are a homogeneous inspiration gallery with no measured downstream purpose.
- The board launders contaminated/derivative sources or instructs the builder to reproduce
  a page's visual skin.

## Why this matters

Research is complete when it reduces consequential uncertainty. Counting captures rewards
collection theater; coverage, independence, and transfer boundaries make the evidence usable.
