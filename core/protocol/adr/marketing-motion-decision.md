# Marketing motion decision

## Status

Accepted for harness v2.

## Decision

An explicit current-user register or motion lock is authoritative before composition. Do not reinterpret, weaken, or replace it with inferred taste.

When the user has not supplied a lock, marketing direction is autonomous: compare **quiet**, **confident**, and **showpiece** against the lawful reference evidence and select the strongest eligible direction without asking the user to choose. Record the selected direction and the two evidence-backed rejections.

Motion is an eligibility decision, not a decorative preference:

- `one` requires exactly one concept-bearing scene, one lawful high-visual-system reference, and either an approved recipe or a selected lawful positive motion observation.
- `none` requires no signature scene, a lawful high-visual-system static reference, a tried lawful CSS/SVG/static or reduced-motion fallback, and demonstrated static influence plus a template-breaking macro departure.
- A generic desire for quiet, restraint, capability limits, category convention, anti-reference avoidance, fear of slop, or generic performance concern is not an escape hatch for `none`.

The decision must pass blind-signature, narrative, motion-fit, fidelity/decision-fit, and macro-landing gates before publication.

## Consequences

The CLI exposes a read-only art-direction check. Final evidence v2 binds the selected decision and accepts only the eligible `none` or `one` evidence shape. Reference-distance telemetry remains advisory and cannot block publication.
